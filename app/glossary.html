Outsourcing 101

We have gathered the industry terminology for you to explore and study

A

Automation

Automation is the process of using technology to automate repetitive or manual tasks that are typically performed by humans. In the BPO industry, automation is used to improve operational efficiency, reduce costs, and enhance the quality of service provided to clients. By automating tasks such as data entry, document processing, and customer support, BPO companies can increase productivity, reduce errors, and provide faster response times to clients.

Agent

An agent is a representative of a BPO company who interacts with customers on behalf of a client company. Agents are typically responsible for providing customer service, sales, and technical support. They may communicate with customers via phone, email, or chat, and are trained to handle a variety of inquiries and issues. Good agents are essential to the success of a BPO company, as they represent the face of the client company and are responsible for maintaining positive customer relationships.

Attrition

Attrition refers to the rate at which employees leave a BPO company, typically due to dissatisfaction with working conditions, pay, or other factors. High attrition rates can be a major challenge for BPO companies, as they can lead to decreased productivity, increased training costs, and a loss of institutional knowledge. BPO companies often invest in programs to improve employee satisfaction and retention, such as employee engagement initiatives, training and development programs, and competitive compensation and benefits packages.

Analytics

Analytics is the process of using data analysis to identify patterns and insights that can be used to improve business processes and decision-making. In the BPO industry, analytics is used to optimize operations, improve customer service, and provide clients with insights into their business operations. BPO companies use analytics tools to analyze data such as call volumes, customer feedback, and performance metrics, and use this information to make data-driven decisions about staffing, training, and process improvements.

Audit

An audit is an independent review of a BPO company's operations, financial records, and internal controls, often conducted by a third-party auditor. Audits are typically conducted to ensure compliance with regulatory requirements, identify areas for improvement, and provide assurance to clients that the BPO company is operating effectively and efficiently. BPO companies may be audited for compliance with industry-specific standards, such as PCI DSS (Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard), or for adherence to client-specific requirements. The audit process typically involves a review of policies and procedures, documentation, and interviews with key personnel.

B

Business Continuity Planning (BCP)

BCP is the process of creating a plan to ensure that critical business functions can continue to operate in the event of a disruption or disaster. In the BPO industry, BCP is important to ensure that client operations can continue uninterrupted in the event of an outage, natural disaster, or other unforeseen event. BCP typically includes provisions for data backup and recovery, alternative work locations, and communication plans.

Business Process Mapping

Business process mapping is the process of creating a visual representation of a business process in order to identify opportunities for improvement. In the BPO industry, process mapping is used to optimize operations and improve efficiency. By analyzing the flow of work, identifying bottlenecks, and streamlining processes, BPO companies can reduce costs, improve quality, and enhance the customer experience.

Business Process Outsourcing (BPO)

BPO refers to the practice of outsourcing non-core business functions to third-party providers. These functions can include back-office operations such as payroll, accounting, and customer service, as well as front-office functions such as sales, marketing, and IT support. BPO is a popular business model for companies looking to reduce costs, increase efficiency, and access specialized expertise.

Back Office

The back office refers to the administrative functions of a business that are not directly customer-facing, such as data entry, billing, and accounting. In the BPO industry, back-office functions are often outsourced to third-party providers in order to reduce costs and improve efficiency. BPO companies may offer a variety of back-office services, including data entry, document processing, accounting, and HR administration.

Bench Strength

Bench strength refers to the availability and readiness of skilled staff to handle additional workload or projects. In the BPO industry, bench strength is important to ensure that a company has the capacity to handle fluctuations in demand and new business opportunities. BPO companies often maintain a pool of trained staff who are not currently assigned to a project, but who can be quickly mobilized as needed. By maintaining strong bench strength, BPO companies can provide fast, high-quality service to clients while maintaining profitability.
